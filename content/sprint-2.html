Affinity Diagram

SPRINT 2Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet

Goal

Synthesize research from user interviews to gain insights about sensemaking for online shopping

Deliverable


Affinity diagram from interpretation notes

We wanted to evaluate different patterns of behavior for foraging, synthesizing and decision phases of shopping sensemaking, which we felt an affinity diagram would assist with. The diagram would also allow us to identify breakdowns and possible design opportunities found across research for further explorations


Process

We began the sprint by trying to map out the landscape of online shopping. The survey conducted hinted that people behaved differently when shopping online, and we were interested in elucidating these different behaviors. Based on our initial observations, and guided by suggestions from Niki, we had three inchoate online shopping types which we sought to explore with in-depth interviews. These types were: satisficers, or someone who does not need to do extensive research online to make a purchase; optimizers, or someone who is willing to do some research (within reason) in order to get a better purchase; and super-optimizers, or those who spend an outsized amount of time and are the primary creators of artifacts in the space.

We conducted interviews with 18 users about their recent electronic product purchases. Initially, we were going to divide our interviews evenly between the three shopper types; ultimately, we decided to interview any users who had recently purchased an electronic product because we weren't able to draw clear lines among those three user groups. We chose to focus on purchases within the electronics domain because these are usually larger purchases which our preliminary research identified as having longer processes of online information gathering and sensemaking. In this way, we could better identify pain points through these processes.

We used the data from this round and the previous two rounds of interviews to discover commonalities among online shoppers, as well as pain points that could identify further research or design opportunities. We were able to find patterns such as

I buy stuff because I want to improve the current lifestyle

I want to go through the process with minimal time

My research intensity depends on how much knowledge I have

Through those small patterns, we were able to generate higher insights such as "research is just a way to reach my goal". After we finished the affinity diagram, we discovered these five main insights:

  • Research is just a way to reach my goal
  • I adjust my research process depending on constraints
  • I rely on trustworthy/official platforms
  • I think something could be optimized for me

Our next research steps were guided by the overarching insight that validation and contextualization of information online are primary friction points across all behavior models. We also were eager to use the data from interviews to map out different models for online shopping to guide our future designs.
