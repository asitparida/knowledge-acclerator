The problem statement

Establishing the context

Buying products online, whether you’re looking at wine, comic books, speakers or lipstick, has several pain points that you’ve probably experienced in your online research journey. You may have trouble knowing the right keywords to search for, you may not be able to try the product out in person, and you may find it difficult to contextualize the product to your lifestyle.

Sensemaking

It is the process of researching online. It is the ability or attempt to make sense of an ambiguous situation. Online sense making can be broken down into these 3 simple steps:

  • Collecting information
  • Summarizing information
  • Artifact creation (maybe)

The domain of online sensemaking has many different aspects

Artifacts

There’s also a concept of artifacts in sensemaking. This refers to capturing your thoughts in a structured manner so you can share with others. Artifacts can be articles, reviews, YouTube videos, Google docs, Excel sheets… artifacts come in many forms.

Most people are what we would call “consumers” -- people who gather information to more quickly make a decision on a purchase on their own. Those who write reviews, make YouTube tutorials, send out Google docs are what we call “contributors” -- people who create and possibly share artifacts.

Next steps

Over the course of 5 sprints, we narrowed down our key questions, morphing our domain scope and ultimately landed on: How might we enable contributors to create better artifacts? This is the question we will take into the summer.
